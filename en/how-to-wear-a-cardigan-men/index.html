How should men wear a cardigan?

By Maxime Coste• June 27, 2026• 6 min read

The cardigan carries an unfair reputation as a grandfather's garment. Wrongly so. Cut well and paired right, this buttoned knit is one of the most elegant and flexible mid-season pieces, halfway between a classic jumper and a light jacket. The only question left is how a man should wear a cardigan without looking either sloppy or over-dressed.

The cardigan: between jumper and jacket

What sets the cardigan apart are its buttons down the front: open it, close it, layer it. That modularity is why it works over a shirt as well as a t-shirt. Where the men's polo shirt owns the summer, the cardigan takes over as the temperature drops, in autumn and early winter.

Choosing well: knit, fabric and fit

It all starts with the knit. Fine merino wool is the most versatile option, warm without bulk. Cashmere, pricier, offers unmatched softness and quality if you want to invest. Chunky knit gives a more casual, rustic feel, great at the weekend but less suited to the office. Avoid pure acrylic, which pills fast and holds heat poorly.

On fit, the cardigan should follow the silhouette without squeezing: sleeves stop at the wrist, the hem covers the waistband without going much lower. A model too long or too loose weighs the whole look down. Favour a clean cut; that is what separates the elegant cardigan from the shapeless one.

Colours to favour

Navy is, again, the best starting point: a navy cardigan pairs with jeans as easily as with dress trousers. Charcoal grey and black follow for a darker, urban register. Camel, burgundy or bottle green bring warmth to the cold season. The rule: a cardigan in a sober colour is easier to wear, while bright shades are for those who already have the basics down.

How to style your cardigan

The cardigan pairs in several ways depending on the layer underneath and the bottoms you choose. Two broad families of styles and their variations:

How to wear a cardigan by occasion
StyleUnderneath + bottomsWhen
Dressed-down smartShirt + wool trousers, derbiesOffice, dinner, autumn
CasualWhite t-shirt + raw jeans, sneakersWeekend
Cosy winterFine roll-neck + chinos, bootsWinter

Over a shirt, the cardigan beats a jacket at the office. Over a t-shirt and jeans, it lifts a look that would otherwise stay too plain. And over a fine roll-neck, it builds a warm, sharp outfit for cold days. If you are after the right bottoms, the men's chinos remain an obvious partner, from beige to navy.

Mistakes to avoid

The cardigan too long, hanging over the thighs. Every button done up to the top, which stiffens the outfit (leave one or two open). The low-grade fabric that pills within a month. And piling on thick layers that erase the silhouette. When in doubt, return to the basics: a navy cardigan in fine wool, worn open, over a light shirt, never disappoints.

Frequently asked questions

What fabric should you choose for a men's cardigan?

Fine merino wool for versatility, cashmere for softness and quality if the budget allows. Avoid pure acrylic, which pills and holds heat poorly. Chunky knit suits casual looks mainly.

How do you wear a cardigan without looking old?

Choose a clean cut that follows the silhouette, a sober colour like navy, and leave one or two buttons open. Worn over a t-shirt and jeans, it instantly looks modern.

When should you wear a cardigan?

Mostly in autumn and early winter, as a mid-season layer. A fine knit also works over a shirt on cool summer evenings.

Cardigan or classic jumper?

The cardigan offers more flexibility thanks to its buttons: open or close it to suit the temperature. A closed jumper is a touch warmer but layers less easily.
