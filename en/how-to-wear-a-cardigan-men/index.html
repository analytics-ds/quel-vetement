How should men wear a cardigan?

By Maxime Coste• June 27, 2026• 6 min read

How to wear a cardigan without looking like your grandfather? Treat it as a jacket, not a jumper: a fine-gauge knit in a sober colour (navy, charcoal, camel), worn over a light shirt or a white tee, with raw denim or plain trousers. Buttons open at the top, one or two, always. Everything else follows from that base. And trust me, this piece deserves far better than its reputation.

The short version

  • Fabric: fine merino wool for daily wear, cashmere if your budget stretches past $250, pure acrylic never.
  • Colours: navy first, grey and camel next. Dark shades go with everything.
  • Underneath: a shirt at the office, a white tee at the weekend, a thin roll neck in winter.
  • Below: raw denim, chinos or flannel trousers. Never joggers.
  • Buttons: open, or fastened two thirds of the way. Never up to the collar.
  • Fit: the hem covers your belt, the sleeves end at the wrist bone.

A military garment turned wardrobe staple

The cardigan takes its name from James Brudenell, 7th Earl of Cardigan, the British major general who led the Charge of the Light Brigade at Balaclava in 1854, and it is modelled on the knitted wool waistcoats British officers wore during the Crimean War. Coco Chanel pulled it into womenswear in the 1920s. Quite a journey for a soldier's knit. These days the piece keeps coming back on menswear runways, pushed by luxury houses and high-street brands alike.

What sets it apart from other knitwear is the buttons. You open it, close it, layer it. That flexibility makes it the best mid-season piece there is, from September to November and again from March to May. Where the polo shirt owns the summer, the cardigan takes over once the thermometer drops below 60 °F (15 °C).

How to choose a cardigan: gauge, fabric, fit

Start with the knit. Fine gauge (12 gauge or higher) slips under a jacket and works at the office. Chunky knit (3 or 5 gauge) replaces the jacket entirely and reads more rugged. Both have their place, just not on the same day. If you are buying your first one, go fine.

On fabric, merino wool, from a sheep breed that originated on the Iberian Peninsula and is prized for its very fine fleece, gives the best warmth-to-weight ratio: a fibre of roughly 18 microns, soft, breathable, no itch. Wool also absorbs up to 30 % of its weight in moisture without feeling wet, which is why you sweat less in it than in synthetics. Expect $70 to $140 for decent merino. Cashmere costs two to three times more but wins on lightness. Read labels carefully: an 80 % wool, 20 % polyamide blend ages better than pure acrylic, which pills within a month. Weight is a clue too: a good fine-knit cardigan sits around 300 to 400 grams, not 150.

Now the fit. The hem covers the belt without reaching your thighs, sleeves stop at the wrist, shoulders sit clean. A small error in length wrecks the silhouette faster than a bad colour ever will. Always try it buttoned: if the knit pulls between two buttons, size up. Judge the shoulder seam, not the logo. The brand matters less than people think, a solid high-street label does the job as well as a big name when the fabric is right. One last thing, care: wool cycle at 30 °C (86 °F) maximum, dry flat, and the piece will last five to ten years.

Which colours work best?

Navy, no contest. A navy cardigan pairs with jeans, beige chinos, grey trousers: it is the colour that pays the purchase back. Charcoal and black come next, more urban, then the warm autumn tones (camel, burgundy, bottle green). Ecru and lighter shades look sharp in spring over dark denim. Save the loud colours for later, they only work once the basics are in place. For combinations that hold together, our guide on how to match colours in an outfit lays out the full method.

How to style a cardigan: four outfits that work

Think in layers: something thin underneath, the cardigan in the middle, a coat on top if winter insists. Keep the layering legible, two visible layers at most. Here are four concrete examples, from dressed-up to laid-back:

Over a shirt, the cardigan replaces the blazer at the office and loses nothing in polish. For stricter settings, fasten it two thirds of the way and pick chinos or wool trousers; the whole outfit stays sharp all day.

Over a white tee, it upgrades a plain look in ten seconds. Wear it open then, with raw jeans and clean sneakers. And over a thin roll neck it builds a warm, neat second layer for cold days, my personal January uniform.

Mistakes to avoid

Knowing how to wear a cardigan mostly means knowing what not to do. The overlong model that drops to mid-thigh. Every button fastened up to the collar, instant schoolmaster effect. Bargain acrylic that pills. Three stacked layers of knitwear that erase the silhouette. One final tip: when in doubt, fall back on the navy knit, light shirt and beige chino trio. Different settings, same result, it works.

Frequently asked questions

Which fabric should you choose for a men's cardigan?

Fine merino wool for versatility ($70 to $140), cashmere for softness if the budget allows. A wool-majority blend is acceptable, pure acrylic is not: it pills and traps little warmth. Chunky knits belong to casual outfits only.

How do you wear a cardigan without looking old?

A clean cut that follows the body, a sober colour like navy, one or two buttons left open at the top. Over a white tee and raw denim, the piece instantly reads modern. Shapeless knits and piled-up layers are what age an outfit, not the garment itself.

When should you wear a cardigan?

Mostly in autumn and early winter, when temperatures sit between 40 and 60 °F (5 to 15 °C). A fine knit also works over a shirt on cool summer evenings, in the mountains for example.

Cardigan or regular jumper?

The cardigan gives you more flexibility thanks to its buttons: open it or close it as the temperature shifts, take it off without wrecking your hair. A closed jumper runs slightly warmer but layers less easily. Both earn their spot in a wardrobe.
