How should men wear chinos?

By Maxime Coste• June 23, 2026• 7 min read

Chinos sit in nearly every man's wardrobe, and they usually get worn the same way, six days out of seven, without much thought. A shame. Picked well and styled right, this cotton trouser works as nicely on a Friday at the office as at a family lunch or a summer wedding. Here is how to wear them without looking like you grabbed the first pair you found.

Why chinos remain a wardrobe staple

Chinos come from late-19th-century military kit, cut in sturdy cotton twill. That stuck: a straight or lightly tapered cut, a fabric that is light yet structured, discreet pockets. Where the men's polo shirt dresses the top half effortlessly, the chino does the same job below the waist. It sits right between jeans and suit trousers, which explains its range.

The real question is not whether to own a pair, but how to dodge the three classic mistakes: a cut too wide that shortens the silhouette, a colour that clashes with your shoes, and a fabric too thin that creases the moment you sit down.

Fit first

Before colour, before the brand, look at the fit. A good chino falls clean over the thigh, tapers slightly toward the ankle and stops just above the shoe, with a single small break or none at all. Too long and it bunches. Too short and it turns into cropped trousers by accident.

For sizing, use a simple test: you should be able to slide a flat hand into the waistband, not your fist. Slimmer men suit a slim or tapered cut. Broader builds breathe better in a straight cut, which stays sharp without squeezing. A quality chino in dense cotton twill ages far better than a cheap, flimsy weave.

Colours that work (and the ones men forget)

Beige and navy cover 80% of situations. Start there if you are building from scratch. Beige pairs with almost anything; navy reads smart without the stiffness of suit trousers. Then come khaki, light grey, burgundy in autumn, off-white in summer.

The rule I always give: the lighter the chino, the more forgiving it is. Beige works with a dark top as well as a light one. A bold-coloured chino needs a neutral top so the outfit does not overload. For a sharper look, go tonal: sand chino, ecru men's cardigan, and the outfit instantly gains depth.

Shoes: the pairing that changes everything

Leather stays the reference: a pair of brown derbies or loafers dresses up beige chinos instantly. For a looser register, clean white leather sneakers do the job, as long as they are minimal. Chunky running shoes, on the other hand, break the line.

Three outfits to copy by occasion

At the office (smart casual)

Navy chinos, a pale-blue oxford shirt tucked in, a leather belt matched to brown derbies. Add an unstructured jacket if the weather allows. Understated, never stiff.

The weekend

Beige chinos, a heavy white t-shirt or plain polo, leather sneakers. Turn the hem up once to show the ankle when it is warm. The easiest outfit to get right.

For a daytime wedding

Yes, chinos work at a countryside or summer wedding. Go for light beige or deep blue, add a dress shirt, a blazer and loafers. The look stays sharp without the weight of a dark suit in full sun.

Three rules to never get it wrong again

If you only keep the essentials, keep these three. One: buy less, but better, to find clothes that last several seasons. Two: stick to two or three colours at first, you will multiply combinations without thinking. Three: mind the rest of the outfit too, because a chino is judged just as much by what sits above it and on your feet. For a balanced look, check what you already own before buying more.

The details that make the difference

A clean turn-up rather than a hasty roll. A belt whose leather echoes the shoes. Pockets left empty so they do not distort the thigh line. Small things, but stacked together they separate the man who "puts on a chino" from the one who actually wears it. Still unsure about the top half? Frequently asked questions

Which chino colour should you buy first?

Beige, without hesitation. It pairs with any top, light or dark, and moves from office to weekend. Navy comes next for slightly dressier occasions.

Can you wear chinos to a wedding?

Yes, for a daytime, countryside or summer wedding. Light-beige or deep-blue chinos with a dress shirt, blazer and leather loafers stay elegant and far more comfortable than a dark suit in the sun.

Which shoes go with beige chinos?

Brown loafers or derbies for a polished finish, white leather sneakers for a casual version. Avoid technical sports trainers, which break the silhouette.

How do you know if your chinos fit?

You should be able to slide a flat hand into the waistband, not your fist. The hem stops just above the shoe with one small break, and the thigh stays clean without pulling.
