How should men wear shorts?

By Maxime Coste• June 30, 2026• 6 min read

In short: wearing shorts well comes down to four decisions. A hem that sits 3 to 8 cm (roughly 1 to 3 inches) above the knee, a trouser-grade fabric (cotton twill, linen), a top dressier than the bottom, and a clean pair of summer shoes worn with no visible socks.

That is the quick answer. Yet every summer, the same scene plays out: men who dress carefully from October to May switch to swim shorts and slides the moment it hits 30 degrees, just to buy bread. Shorts are not a surrender. They are a garment you pick with the same criteria as a pair of chinos: cut, fabric, colour, and a little common sense about the occasion.

How to wear shorts: the essentials in five rules

Here are a few rules that settle 90 percent of summer situations:

  • Length: hem 3 to 8 cm above the knee, never below it.
  • Cut: straight or slightly tailored, waist sitting on the hips, no bulging cargo pockets.
  • Fabric: cotton twill around 200 to 250 g/m² for the city, linen or linen-cotton on holiday.
  • Colours: beige, sand, navy and khaki as the base, one discreet pattern at a time.
  • Around them: a polo or light shirt, clean shoes, invisible socks or none.

Each of these points deserves two minutes of attention. Let's take them in order, from the most important to the most cosmetic.

Length, decision number one

Everything happens between 3 and 8 cm above the knee. That window lengthens the leg, frees the calf without exposing the whole thigh, and works on the vast majority of body types. Below the knee, shorts compress the silhouette and bring back memories of school uniforms. Go much shorter, say 13 cm and more above the knee, and the result turns beachy and demands trained legs. It is no accident that brands have been shortening their cuts since the early 2010s: the above-the-knee window flatters the most people.

What length of shorts for your body type?

A practical fitting-room test: standing, arms by your sides, the hem should land roughly at your fingertips. Shorter build? Aim for the top of the window (7-8 cm above the knee), it visually stretches the leg. Tall and slim, you can drop to 3-4 cm without risk. Strong thighs, look for a straight cut with 2 to 3 cm of ease around the thigh rather than anything skin-tight. And if you hesitate between two lengths, take the shorter one: slightly long shorts can be cuffed, the reverse is impossible.

Shorts or Bermuda shorts: what is the difference?

The difference is a matter of centimetres: Bermuda shorts stop at the knee or barely above, shorts stop at mid-thigh or a little lower. Their history is a good story too. The invention is credited to Nathaniel Coxon, a Bermudian tea shop owner who hemmed his employees' uniform trousers in 1914 to cope with the summer heat; the British Army then adopted the style for tropical climates, as the Wikipedia page on Bermuda shorts recounts. In Bermuda they are still considered proper business attire, worn with knee-length socks, a shirt, tie and blazer.

Which one should you pick? My take is blunt: well-cut shorts age better than Bermudas, which cut the leg at the worst possible spot and shorten everyone except the very tall. If you insist on Bermudas, go tailored, plain, and keep the rest of the outfit very sharp. In every other case, shorts ending above the knee remain the smart default.

Fabrics and colours: pick shorts like you pick trousers

The chino short in cotton twill remains the city reference: structured, opaque, crisp, usually 25 to 60 euros for decent quality. Linen and linen-cotton blends win on freshness for holidays, in the same spirit as a linen shirt. The fibre has real credentials: woven from flax, it dries faster than cotton, which is exactly why it feels comfortable in hot weather, as the Wikipedia entry on linen details. Seersucker and lightweight cotton round out the summer wardrobe, provided you stick to one discreet pattern (fine stripes, micro-checks).

On colour, trouser logic applies. Beige, sand, navy and khaki form the base and pair with almost any top. Off-white and olive green complete the set. Tropical prints belong on swim shorts, not city shorts. To build around them, the rules from our guide on matching colours in an outfit hold in short form as well as long: two neutrals, one accent colour maximum.

What top should you wear with shorts?

Wearing shorts in the city without looking like you wandered off a beach is first of all a question of the top half. Shorts expose your legs, so the rest has to reassure. Think of it as a slider: the more relaxed the bottom, the sharper the top. A well-cut polo shirt, a light shirt with short or rolled sleeves, a solid heavyweight t-shirt (180 g/m² minimum, or it sags after the first wash). Tank tops, football jerseys and big-logo tees drag the whole outfit towards the beach or the stadium.

Tucking depends on the top. Polos and t-shirts stay untucked over shorts, as long as the hem stops below the waistband, not at mid-thigh. A light shirt can be tucked for a more constructed look, with a braided belt that forgives waist variations. Comfort should never read as sloppiness: that nuance is the whole game with a summer outfit.

Which shoes go with shorts?

This is where everything is won or lost when you wear shorts day to day. A correct shorts outfit with bad shoes fails; the reverse often saves the day. The table sums up the four reliable options and their register.

The subject of white sneakers deserves its own guide, but the short version fits in one line: kept white, low profile, zero oversized sole. As for invisible socks, they are non-negotiable. A tennis sock peeking out ruins any effort made higher up.

Shorts at the office and formal occasions: the limits

A dressy dinner, a professional meeting, a ceremony: shorts have no place there, even at 35 degrees. At work the question is not even stylistic: in France, an employer can legally ban shorts at the office, a position upheld by the supreme court in a 2003 ruling, and most workplaces elsewhere are no more forgiving. In those situations, lightweight linen or breathable cotton trousers handle the heat without sacrificing the register. That is the only hard no. Everywhere else, from the Sunday market to evening drinks, well-chosen shorts hold their own.

Three outfit examples with shorts

For a city look, the safest example: sand chino shorts, navy polo, white leather sneakers, a watch on a fabric strap. Sober, crisp, sharp without visible effort. It works from 25 to 60 years old.

On holiday, switch to the linen register: slightly tailored ecru shorts, a striped shirt open over a white t-shirt, espadrilles. The good version of relaxed, the one you can wear from breakfast to dinner on the harbour.

For a summer evening, go one notch up: navy shorts with an impeccable cut, a light white poplin shirt tucked in, sockless loafers, braided belt. That is the outfit that proves dressed-up shorts can be learned in a single season. None of it requires a big budget: count 100 to 150 euros for the full look at serious entry-level prices.

Frequently asked questions

How long should shorts be?

Between 3 and 8 cm above the knee for most body types. Shorter demands athletic legs and confidence. Below the knee, the silhouette instantly shrinks.

Can you wear shorts in the city?

Yes, provided you raise everything else a notch: crisp chino shorts, a polo or a light shirt, clean shoes. Swim shorts and slides stay at the beach.

Which shoes go with shorts?

Espadrilles, white leather sneakers, sockless loafers or good-quality sandals. High socks with smart shorts is a stylist's gamble, not a default.

Are denim shorts a good idea?

Cut clean above the knee and not frayed, they can work on holiday. For the city, chino or linen shorts give a more grown-up look.
