How should men wear a linen shirt?

By Maxime Coste• July 1, 2026• 7 min read

A men's linen shirt works with a fabric weight of 130 to 170 gsm, a slightly relaxed fit, natural colours (ecru, beige, light blue) and lightweight trousers below. Those are the starting numbers. Knowing how to wear a linen shirt then comes down to accepting the fabric for what it is: a living cloth that creases with panache and that, from 28°C (82°F) upwards, dethrones the cotton shirt, even the office white shirt. The complete guide, from the shop floor to evening plans.

In brief: the five-point cheat sheet

In summary, here are the few rules that condense the rest of this guide:

  • Ideal weight: 130 to 170 gsm, opaque without feeling heavy.
  • Fit: neither clinging nor baggy, shoulders in place, room through the chest.
  • Starter colours: ecru, beige, light blue; almond green and terracotta later.
  • Down below: chino trousers, linen trousers or raw denim, sandals banned in town.
  • Care: wash at 30°C, dry on a hanger, steam iron on the collar only.

Why linen rules the hottest days

Linen fibre absorbs up to 20% of its weight in moisture without looking wet, dries faster than cotton and lets air move against the skin. A permanent feeling of coolness, where cotton holds out for twenty minutes. The fibre conducts heat away from the body and can be up to three times stronger than cotton, as the Wikipedia entry on linen details. It gets better with every wash instead of wearing out.

A geography note in passing: roughly three quarters of the world's long flax fibre comes from France, Belgium and the Netherlands, and 85 to 90% of Europe's flax acreage sits in France, according to the Alliance for European Flax-Linen and Hemp. Buying a quality linen shirt often means buying European without knowing it.

Its creased reputation is deserved. But reading that as a flaw misses the point: a light, even crease is part of the garment's look, exactly like the fades on raw denim. A linen shirt pressed flat loses its natural character, and with it the reason to own one.

How to choose a linen shirt: weight, fit, colour

The fabric weight first: below 120 gsm, the shirt turns transparent and fragile. Between 130 and 170 gsm you hit the balance point, decent opacity, soft drape, real durability. Some shirts blend linen with cotton: a fair compromise that creases less, at the cost of some breathability and a little comfort in serious heat.

The fit next: linen needs room to breathe, literally. A slightly relaxed cut, shoulders where they belong but chest not clinging, gives the ideal drape. Too slim, and the shirt marks every crease in the worst spot. Too loose, and it drifts into beach pyjamas.

Colour last: off-white, ecru, beige and light blue form the classic quartet. Washed shades (almond green, pale terracotta, fine stripes) work remarkably on this natural fabric, which catches light differently from smooth cotton. Pure white demands a serious weight or transparency wins. Before buying, a detour through our method for matching colours in an outfit saves you from picking at random: it applies word for word to the summer wardrobe.

Four looks that work, from the beach to the office

By the sea or on a long weekend: ecru shirt worn untucked, sand-coloured chino shorts, espadrilles. The perfect summer trio, provided the shirt hem is straight and short.

In town in serious heat: light blue shirt tucked into beige chino trousers, leather loafers worn sockless, woven belt. The look stays sharp enough for the office while breathing, and stands in for the classic suit from June to September.

Evenings on a terrace: dark shirt (navy, pine green) over light linen trousers, sleeves rolled in two clean turns, a watch on a fabric or steel strap. The dressiest outfit this cloth allows, ideal for dinner without a jacket.

And how do you wear a linen shirt when the dress code demands a jacket? Under a cotton blazer, collar open; the unstructured linen suit remains the only tailored outfit genuinely wearable at 32°C (90°F). Just skip the structured wool jacket, whose rigour clashes with the soft drape. Over raw denim, though, it works from spring to autumn.

Care: simple, on two conditions

Wash at 30°C, gentle spin, no aggressive stain removers, then dry on a hanger: the weight of the water smooths the shirt almost on its own. The tumble dryer is enemy number one, it shrinks the fibre and sets the creases. For dressier occasions, a steam pass over collar, cuffs and placket is enough; the rest of the garment keeps its movement.

One last point, rarely made: linen likes rotation. Two shirts worn alternately age better than one worn on repeat, and the fibre recovers its loft between outings. Fashion moves on, a good linen shirt lasts ten years.

Frequently asked questions

Should you iron a linen shirt?

Lightly, and never flat. A steam pass on the collar and cuffs is enough: the slightly creased drape is part of linen's charm. A perfectly smooth linen shirt loses its natural feel.

Are linen shirts see-through?

Light weights in white can be. Pick linen of at least 130 gsm, or ecru and coloured shades that conceal better. An invisible undershirt settles the question at the office.

Linen shirt tucked in or out?

Out over shorts or chino trousers in summer, if the hem is straight and short. Tucked in with dressier trousers, where it replaces the cotton shirt as soon as the heat arrives.

How do you wash a linen shirt?

At 30°C, gentle spin, dried on a hanger. Linen dries fast and gets stronger with washing, but the tumble dryer makes it shrink and breaks its fibres.
