How should men wear a white shirt?

By Maxime Coste• June 26, 2026• 6 min read

If the men's wardrobe had to keep a single piece, the debate would be over quickly. The white shirt crosses decades, dress codes and seasons without ageing a day: under a ceremony suit on Saturday, open over jeans on Sunday. Its reputation as a basic hides real decisions, though: the collar, the fit, the fabric, and the art of not wearing it like a uniform.

The collar decides everything

The spread collar (semi-cutaway depending on the maker) remains the most versatile: moderately spread points, it holds up with a tie as well as open. The cutaway collar, wider, dresses up further and calls for a fuller tie knot. The button-down collar, inherited from American polo shirts, casualises the piece: perfect under a jumper, with jeans, or worn alone with rolled sleeves.

One simple principle for the morning choice: the stiffer and more spread the collar, the dressier the shirt. And a collar that curls or lifts at the points betrays a tired shirt, whatever it cost.

Fit and fabric: the difference between wearing and floating

A successful white shirt follows the chest without clinging: shoulders at the bone, a fist of ease between fabric and stomach, a length that stays tucked when you raise your arms. Smooth, slightly lustrous poplin plays the dressy card; oxford, thicker and textured, makes an everyday shirt that ages magnificently; twill, soft and supple, barely creases. In summer, linen takes over as soon as poplin sticks.

On transparency, let us settle it once and for all: under a slightly fine white shirt, an invisible undershirt in heather grey or skin tone disappears completely, where a white undershirt shows like a poster. V-neck or collarless, never a visible crew neck.

Five ways to wear it, from dressiest to most relaxed

Under a suit with a tie: the ceremony version, smooth poplin, spread or cutaway collar. Smart casual: tucked into flannel trousers or chinos, no tie, two buttons open at most. With raw denim: the contrast formula that has worked for decades, sleeves rolled in two clean turns. Under knitwear: a button-down collar peeking from a navy jumper or a cardigan, the white brightening the face. Untucked over chinos in summer: only if the hem is straight and short, polished-holiday spirit.

Keeping it white: the care that changes everything

A greyed or yellowed white shirt cancels every styling effort. The protocol: treat the collar and cuffs with stain soap before every wash, machine at 40 degrees maximum, a gentle oxygen whitener once a month, air drying. The tumble dryer yellows fibres and sets stains. And at the first sign of collar wear, the shirt gets recycled into a painting shirt: white does not forgive fatigue.

One last buying tip: two average white shirts are worth less than one excellent one. It is the piece where fabric quality shows most, precisely because there is no colour or pattern to create a diversion.

Frequently asked questions

White shirt tucked in or untucked?

Tucked in whenever the outfit is dressy or the shirt has long rounded tails. Untucked only if the hem is straight and stops mid-fly, over jeans or relaxed chinos.

Which collar should you choose on a white shirt?

The classic spread collar is the most versatile: it works with or without a tie. A button-down collar reads more casual, perfect under a jumper or with jeans.

What should you wear under a white shirt?

A close-fitting invisible undershirt, heather grey or skin-toned, never pure white and never with a visible crew neck. It absorbs sweat and kills transparency.

How do you keep a white shirt truly white?

Wash at 40 degrees maximum with a gentle oxygen whitener from time to time, air dry, and treat the collar with stain soap before every wash.
