T-shirts and polos

How should men wear a polo shirt?

By Maxime Coste• June 25, 2026• 6 min read

We all think we know how to wear a polo shirt. That is exactly the trap: between the baggy early-2000s cut and the oversized logo, the men's polo gets botched more often than you would expect. Chosen well, it is one of the most useful pieces in a summer wardrobe, halfway between a t-shirt and a shirt. Here is how to give it real presence.

The polo: deceptively simple

A good polo comes down to three things: fabric, cut and colour. The rest follows. In cotton piqué it holds its shape and breathes; in a finer knit it edges toward a smart register. Where the men's chinos structure the bottom of an outfit, the polo bridges casual and dressy up top. That is what makes it a hot-weather staple.

Choosing the right polo

Start with the cut. A polo should be fitted without clinging: the shoulder seam lands on the joint, the sleeve stops mid-bicep, and the hem ends around the mid-fly so it works tucked or untucked. Too long or too wide and it flattens the silhouette. That is mistake number one.

On sleeves, two schools. Short sleeves in cotton piqué for the classic summer use. Long sleeves, rarer, for an elegant mid-season look under a jacket. For sizing, trust the fabric: a slightly heavier piqué ages better than a thin knit that warps in the wash. Whatever your build, a clean cut flatters more than poorly managed volume.

Colours and patterns that work

The navy polo is the obvious starting point: it suits every complexion and pairs with everything. Then come white, marl grey, bottle green, burgundy. A dark navy polo always reads dressier than a light shade, handy when you want a serious look without a tie.

Patterns and prints call for more care: a fine breton stripe goes anywhere, a bold tropical print belongs on holiday. The simple rule: if the pattern catches the eye before the face, choose something else.

What to wear it with, office to weekend

The polo adapts to almost any occasion depending on what sits below. A few combinations and ways to wear it:

For an office look, a plain polo tucked into suit trousers with a leather belt stands in nicely for a shirt on scorching days. For something looser, wear it out over chinos or jeans, and drop the belt.

Mistakes to avoid

The collar that curls up, from poor pressing or poor quality. Both buttons left wide open, which looks sloppy. The over-long polo left hanging on the hips. And the oversized logo, which dates any outfit. Avoid those four pitfalls and you are already a cut above. When it cools down, a light men's cardigan layers perfectly on top.

Frequently asked questions

Can you wear a polo to the office?

Yes, as long as it is plain, fitted and in a sober colour (navy, white, grey). Tucked into suit trousers with a leather belt, it replaces a shirt on very hot days, in a smart-casual register.

Should you tuck a polo in?

It depends on the register. Tucked in, the polo looks dressier and suits the office. Untucked, it is more casual and ideal at the weekend, provided it stops around the mid-fly and no lower.

Which polo colour should you buy first?

The navy polo, without hesitation: it suits every complexion and works with any bottoms. White and marl grey then round out a versatile base.

Short sleeves or long sleeves?

Short sleeves in cotton piqué for classic summer wear. Long sleeves, dressier, for mid-season or under an unstructured jacket.
